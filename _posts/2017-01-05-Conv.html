Fig. 1-1 Convolution, Cross-correlation and Auto-correlation

As can be seen, all three operations perform calculations sliding along the "time line". At a particular time instant, it generally performs multiply and integration operations: "Convolution" obtains filtering result for this moment, "cross-correlation" evaluates "similarity" between two different series for this moment (intuitively, align two series by this time instant and evaluate), and "auto-correlation" evaluates "similarity" of a series on its own by "aligning" its own value according to this moment. Sliding over the time line, we obtain continueous or discrete results (depend on the form of "time-line"), e.g., if a series is in the form of matrix, like an image, the time-line will be in discrete 2d-format indicating the row-number and column number respectively.

More on covolution: $$ conv(f,h) = \sum f(t) h(x - t) $$
